About the Book

“How to Win Friends and Influence People” is one of the best-selling self-help books of all time. First published in 1936, Dale Carnegie's work remains a cornerstone of communication, leadership, and relationship-building literature. This website is dedicated to spreading awareness of the book's valuable lessons.

About Dale Carnegie

Dale Carnegie was an American writer, lecturer, and developer of courses in self-improvement, salesmanship, and interpersonal skills. His goal was to help people become more confident, persuasive, and compassionate communicators.

Key Ideas from the Book

  • Show genuine interest in others
  • Smile and use people's names
  • Be a good listener
  • Talk in terms of others’ interests
  • Avoid criticizing, condemning, or complaining
  • Give honest and sincere appreciation

Famous Quotes

“You can make more friends in two months by becoming interested in other people than you can in two years by trying to get other people interested in you.”
“Talk to someone about themselves and they’ll listen for hours.”
“Remember that a person’s name is to that person the sweetest and most important sound in any language.”
